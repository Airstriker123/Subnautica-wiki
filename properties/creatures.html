Fish

Explore the diverse underwater ecosystem of Subnautica and encounter a variety of fascinating fish species:

  • Peeper: Small, colorful fish commonly found near the surface. They can be caught for food and used as a source of hydration.
  • Bladderfish: Bioluminescent fish known for their unique air sacs. These sacs can be harvested to create filtered water, a vital resource for survival.
  • Hoopfish: Graceful fish that can be caught and consumed for sustenance.
  • Stalker: Aggressive predator known for its sharp teeth and tendency to collect metal salvage. Approach with caution.

Leviathans

Encounter massive and awe-inspiring leviathan creatures in the depths of Subnautica:

  • Reaper Leviathan: Large and fearsome predator that can deal massive damage. It is found in various locations, particularly near wrecks and deep trenches.
  • Ghost Leviathan: Ghostly, serpent-like creature that inhabits the Ghost Forest and the Lost River. It possesses a powerful bite and should be avoided or approached with extreme caution.
  • Sea Dragon Leviathan: Enormous, fire-breathing creature that guards the Inactive Lava Zone and the Lava Lakes. It is highly aggressive and poses a significant threat to explorers.
  • Sea Emperor Leviathan: Majestic and ancient creature residing in the Primary Containment Facility. Interacting with it is crucial to the game's storyline.

Aliens

Uncover the mysteries of alien lifeforms in Subnautica:

  • Warper: Alien creature capable of teleportation. It emits a distinctive sound and possesses the ability to warp players out of vehicles.
  • Precursor Race: Discover remnants of an advanced alien civilization known as the Precursors. Learn about their technology, artifacts, and their connection to the planet.
  • Sea Emperor Juveniles: Offspring of the Sea Emperor Leviathan, they play a vital role in the game's story and progression.
  • Sea Dragon Leviathan: While classified as a leviathan, the Sea Dragon Leviathan can also be considered an alien creature due to its unique physiology and behavior.

Where They Are Found

Discover the habitats and locations where different creatures can be found in Subnautica:

  • Peeper: Commonly found in the Safe Shallows and Kelp Forest biomes, especially near the water's surface.
  • Bladderfish: Scattered throughout various biomes, with a higher concentration in the Safe Shallows and Kelp Forest.
  • Hoopfish: Typically found in the Grassy Plateaus and Sparse Reef biomes.
  • Stalker: Primarily located in the Kelp Forest and Grassy Plateaus, often near metal salvage.

How to Avoid Them

Learn strategies to avoid encounters with dangerous creatures in Subnautica:

  • Reaper Leviathan: Stay clear of areas where Reapers are known to inhabit. Traveling in a Seamoth or Cyclops can provide protection and speed.
  • Ghost Leviathan: Keep a safe distance and avoid sudden movements. It is advised to use a Prawn Suit or Seamoth for increased maneuverability.
  • Sea Dragon Leviathan: Stay out of its line of sight and use the environment to your advantage. Seek shelter in caves or use the Cyclops for protection.
  • Warper: Listen for its distinct sound and avoid its teleportation attacks. Staying away from their territory and using Silent Running mode on the Cyclops can help avoid them.
